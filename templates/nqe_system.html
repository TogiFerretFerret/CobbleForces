The NQE (Not Quite Elo) System

CobbleForces uses a custom rating algorithm designed to be asymmetric and inflationary.

Standard Elo is zero-sum (points are only transferred). NQE is positive-sum, meaning high performance is rewarded more than low performance is punished. This ensures that legendary tiers like Yuridanshi (4000+) are achievable over time.

Phase 1: Calculating the Seed

Before any changes are applied, we calculate your Expected Rank (Seed). This is based on the probability of you beating every other opponent in the contest.

Probability(A beats B) = 1 / (1 + 10^((RatingB - RatingA) / 400))
Seed = 1 + Σ Probability(Opponents beat You)

Phase 2: Performance Rating

After the contest, we determine your Performance Rating. This is the rating you effectively performed at during this specific contest.

Using a bisection binary search, we find a rating value that would have made your Seed equal to your Actual Rank.

Phase 3: The Drift Update

Your rating doesn't jump immediately to your performance rating. Instead, it "drifts" toward it using a dynamic Stability Factor (K).

RatingChange = K * (PerformanceRating - CurrentRating) + Bonuses
Dynamic K-Factors:
  • Placement Phase (0-10 contests): K = 0.5 (Rapid climbing).
  • Established Phase (11+ contests): K = 0.2 (Stability).
  • Choke Protection: If you perform worse than your current rating, K is halved (losses hurt less than wins help).

Phase 4: Gamification & Bonuses

To support the "Cobble" flavor and keep the climb engaging, flat bonuses are applied:

  • Protagonist Bonus: Users below 1200 rating get a flat +5 if they solve at least one problem.
  • Speedster Bonus: If your average penalty per solved problem is under 20 minutes, you get a flat +2 and a ⚡ icon in the standings.
  • Activity Streak: Participating in 3 consecutive contests grants a 1.1x multiplier to your rating change.

Phase 5: Rating Compression (The Soft Cap)

To prevent infinite inflation and maintain the prestige of the Crown Perk, ratings above 4000 are compressed:

NewRating = 4000 + (RawNewRating - 4000) / 2

This makes 4000+ an "Exponential Hill"—the higher you go, the more effort is required to stay there.
